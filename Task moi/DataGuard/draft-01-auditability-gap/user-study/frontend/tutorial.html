Tutorial

Each app you see has two parts:

  1. Data Safety panel. A short structured table the developer submitted to Google Play. It lists data the app says it shares, data it collects, and security practices.
  2. Privacy policy. A longer document with the legal description of the same things.

You will make four judgments per app:

  • Sharing — Correct or Incorrect. Does the policy support the sharing claims in the Data Safety panel?
  • Sharing — Complete or Incomplete. Does the policy describe any sharing practice that is missing from the panel?
  • Collection — Correct or Incorrect.
  • Collection — Complete or Incomplete.

You will also rate your confidence (0–100) on each judgment.

Three interface conditions will appear (in a randomised order):

  • C0. Raw artefacts: the Data Safety panel and the full policy text. You scroll through the policy yourself.
  • C1. The Data Safety panel plus the Data Share and Data Collect sections extracted from the policy.
  • C2. Same as C1 plus AI-highlighted candidate evidence and an AI-suggested judgment for each axis. You can accept the suggestion or override it.
