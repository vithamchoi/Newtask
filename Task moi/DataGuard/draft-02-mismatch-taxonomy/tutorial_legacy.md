# Tutorial script — Legacy build
*Estimated time: 8–10 minutes. Read verbatim. Italics = experimenter action.*

---

## Opening (≈1 min)

> "Thank you for joining today. The study is comparing two versions of an audit workbench for Android privacy disclosures. You'll see one version first and the other version after a short break. Both versions present the same task on the same set of apps — you don't need to remember anything specific about which version is which.
>
> Right now you're looking at the *Legacy* version, which is the baseline. After a 5-minute walkthrough I'll let you start the first block of 20 apps. Please ask me anything you want during this walkthrough, but during the actual audit blocks I won't be answering questions about the apps. OK?"

*Wait for verbal acknowledgement.*

---

## What you are auditing (≈2 min)

> "Each app on Google Play publishes two privacy artefacts. On the left of the screen, you see the *Data Safety section* — that's the structured table the developer fills in through the Google Play Console. Each row tells you whether some kind of data is shared with third parties, collected by the app, or governed by a security practice like encryption."

*Point at the left pane.*

> "On the right of the screen, you see an excerpt of the app's *privacy policy* — the free-text document the developer hosts on their own website. The policy is the source of truth: it should be the long-form description of what the structured table claims.
>
> Your task is to read both sides and decide, on four binary axes:
> 1. Is the *sharing* claim correct?
> 2. Is the *sharing* claim complete?
> 3. Is the *collection* claim correct?
> 4. Is the *collection* claim complete?
>
> 'Correct' means the artefacts agree on what is in scope. 'Complete' means the structured side lists everything the policy describes. Use your judgement; there is no formula. Then write a one- or two-sentence rationale and click *Submit verdict*."

---

## The first five apps are a tutorial (≈2 min)

> "The first five apps are tutorial stimuli. The verdict radios are disabled on those apps. Use them to practise reading, scrolling through the policy, and matching it against the table. When you reach the sixth app the radios become active and your verdicts start being recorded.
>
> Take as much time as you want on each app. There is no time pressure. The timer in the bottom-left is for our analysis later; please ignore it.
>
> The rationale field is short — one or two sentences is fine. Don't overthink it: write what you would tell a colleague who asked why you chose that verdict."

---

## What happens after 20 apps (≈1 min)

> "After 20 apps you'll be redirected to a short questionnaire called NASA-TLX. It has six sliders asking how mentally demanding the task was, how rushed you felt, and so on. Move each slider to where you feel it best represents your experience and click *Submit*. There are no right answers.
>
> After the NASA-TLX, you'll get a 5-minute break. Then I'll switch you to the second version of the workbench and you'll do another 20 apps and another NASA-TLX. Then a 5-question exit interview, and you're done."

---

## Compensation (≈0.5 min)

> "Compensation is processed at the end of the session and does not depend on your accuracy. The study is paid for the time you spend, not the verdicts you give. You can withdraw at any point by closing the browser; I'll still process the compensation pro-rata. Any questions before we start?"

*Wait for any questions. Resolve.*

> "Great. I'll switch you into the audit now. The screen will load the first tutorial app. Take your time."

*Click the navigation link on the experimenter laptop. Observe the participant complete the first tutorial app silently. Intervene only if the workbench is not loading.*

---

## Comprehension check (≈0.5 min)

After the first tutorial app loads, ask:

> "Quick check: which four binary verdicts are you giving on each app?"

Expected answer: sharing-correct, sharing-complete, collection-correct, collection-complete.

If the answer is incomplete, walk through the four fieldsets in the bottom bar once more. Mark the comprehension check `PASSED` or `FAILED` on the experimenter form.

---

## Final remark before live block

> "OK, when you reach the sixth app the radios will activate. After that I'll be on call but won't be answering audit-content questions. Good luck."

*Leave the participant to begin Block 1.*
