## What is this study about?
We are studying how people read the privacy information that app stores
show before you install a new app. You will be asked to look at the
privacy section of **four fictional apps** and answer a few short
questions about each. The whole study should take 15–20 minutes.

## What will you be asked to do?
- Read a short consent form (this page).
- Answer 3 short questions about your phone-use background.
- Look at four privacy labels for fictional apps, one at a time.
- For each label: answer one multiple-choice question, three short
  scale questions, and one one-sentence question.
- Answer a short demographic block.

## What information do we collect?
- Your responses to all the questions above.
- The Prolific anonymous participant ID (so we can pay you and
  exclude duplicate participation).
- The time you spend on each screen.
- Within the prototype interface, *which buttons you click and which
  drawers you open*.

We do **not** collect your name, email, real device identifiers, or
any biometric or location data.

## How will the information be used?
- To compare how people understand two versions of an app-store
  privacy label.
- Aggregated, de-identified results will appear in a scientific
  publication and in supplementary research material.
- No individual response will be reported in a way that could be
  linked back to you.

## Are there risks?
This study presents fictional apps in a privacy-information context.
The content does not include shocking or distressing material, but if
you have any concerns about how your own apps handle your data, you
may find some questions thought-provoking. You may withdraw at any
time without penalty.

## How will you be paid?
You will be paid through Prolific in the usual way for a study of this
length, regardless of your answers, provided you complete the study
in good faith.

## Storage and retention
Anonymous responses will be stored on a secured institutional server
for at least 5 years and may be re-used in future analyses of label
design. The de-identified data will be released as supplementary
material with the published paper.

## Your rights
You can stop the study at any time without giving a reason. You may
ask for your data to be deleted **up until the point of
publication** by contacting the researchers via Prolific. After
publication, withdrawal of the de-identified, aggregated data may not
be possible.
