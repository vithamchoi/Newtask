## What is this study about?

We are studying how people compare an app's privacy disclosures with its
privacy policy. You will look at a small number of Android apps and judge
whether the app's short privacy summary is consistent with the longer privacy
policy.

## What will you do?

You will:
1. Read this consent form and agree to participate.
2. Complete a brief demographic and screening questionnaire (≈3 minutes).
3. Watch a tutorial and complete a comprehension check (≈8 minutes).
4. Review 24 apps in three blocks (≈45 minutes total). Each app is shown
   alongside its privacy policy under one of three interface conditions.
5. Complete a short workload questionnaire (NASA-TLX) after each block
   (≈2 minutes each).
6. After the AI-assisted block only, complete a brief trust survey
   (≈2 minutes).
7. Optionally provide free-text feedback.

Total time: about 60–75 minutes.

## What data are collected?

- Your judgments for each app (4 yes/no-style ratings per app).
- Your confidence rating for each judgment (0–100 slider).
- The time you spent on each trial.
- Your responses to NASA-TLX and the trust survey.
- Optional free-text rationales.
- Demographic background (age band, English self-rating, Android-use
  frequency, prior privacy-policy reading frequency, developer-experience
  flag).

No personally identifying information is collected. You are identified only
by a random session token.

## Are there any risks?

The study uses publicly available privacy disclosures. There is no expected
risk beyond normal screen-based reading.

## Will the data be shared?

De-identified per-trial data and analysis scripts will be released alongside
the publication. Free-text rationales are reviewed and de-identified before
release. Your session token is randomly generated and cannot be linked back
to you.

## Can you withdraw?

Yes. You can withdraw at any time without explanation. Any data already
submitted up to that point can be deleted at your request (please contact
the PI with your session token).
