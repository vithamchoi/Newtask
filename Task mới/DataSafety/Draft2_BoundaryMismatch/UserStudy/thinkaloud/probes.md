# Think-aloud — moderator probes

These probes are deployed **only when prompted by the participant's
reading**. Do not run through them as a fixed script. Use them when
the participant pauses, looks puzzled, or skims past something
without commenting.

## On encountering a flag row

- "What do you think this row is telling you?"
- "Is there anything missing from this row that you would want to
  know?"

## On encountering the BLE drawer for the first time

- "What do you think this yellow box is?"
- "What is it telling you that the row above is not?"
- "Whose voice is the box speaking in — the developer, Google, or
  somebody else?"
- "Does the box change what you would do?"

## On the rule clause

- "Could you read that line out loud and then say it back in your
  own words?"
- "What would have to be true for that line to be untrue?" *(this is
  the falsifier probe: are participants able to articulate what
  could break the boundary case?)*

## On the community signal

- "What do you think the percentages mean?"
- "Where would you imagine those numbers come from?"
- "Are they too high, too low, or about right for how you yourself
  would answer?"

## On the counter-factual toggle ("Show what the label would say if
the rule did not apply")

- "What do you expect to happen when you tap this?"
- *(let them tap)*
- "Was that what you expected? What changed?"
- "Would you use this kind of button on a real label?"

## On not opening a drawer

- "You read past that box without opening it — what made you skip
  it?"
- "If you only had time to open one box in the whole label, which one
  would it be?"

## On the contested flag

- "This row has a small chip that says 'boundary $b_X$'. What do you
  think that chip is for?"
- "If you ran the app store, would you put that chip there?"

## Comparative probe (re-look at current condition)

- "Compared to the label you just looked at, what does this one
  tell you?"
- "What does this one not tell you that the previous one told you?"
- "If you could only have one of these two labels in the Play Store,
  which would you choose?"

## Debrief probes

- "If you had to cut one thing from the BLE drawer to keep the label
  shorter, what would you cut?"
- "If you could add one thing to the BLE drawer, what would you
  add?"
- "Was there anything about the drawer that you actively
  disliked?"

## Things to avoid as a moderator

- "Don't you think...?" → leading.
- "Most people say..." → social-proof leading.
- "What about service providers?" → suggests the concept the
  research is trying to surface.
- Reading drawer text aloud for the participant.
